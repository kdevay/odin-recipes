Poached Egg

Description

Poaching eggs is a deceptively complicated task. Thankfully, I have discovered a fool-proof method for making the perfect, teardrop-shaped poached egg every time.

The key to beautiful poached eggs is using deep enough water, swirling the water before adding the egg, and discarding the outer, less-viscous egg white.

The addition of vinegar to the poaching liquid does not effect the flavor, and ensures that egg whites will firm up quickly, further ensuring the teardrop shape.

The whole process takes 15 minutes, and yields one poached egg.

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 2 clean ramekins
  • fine mesh sieve
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 cup of cold water

Steps

  1. Add water and vinegar to a 2-quart saucepan and bring to a simmer.
  2. Crack the egg into the fine mesh sieve over the ramekin, allowing the outer egg white to drip through the sieve.
  3. Transfer the egg from the sieve into the other clean ramekin.
  5. Store the excess egg white in the fridge for use in other recipes.
  6. Using a slotted spoon, vigorously swirl the boiling water in the saucepan until it creates a deep vortex in the middle. (Water depth and velocity of the swirl is key to achieving the teardrop shape)
  7. While the water is still swirling, gently tip the egg out of the ramekin into the water.
  8. Use the slotted spoon to keep the water swirling, carefully avoiding the egg, until the white turns from translucent to opaque.
  9. For runny yolks, cook for 3 minutes. For jammy yolks, cook for 5 minutes.
  10. Remove the egg with the slotted spoon, and dip in cold water to stop the cooking and remove any excess white.
  11. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and enjoy!
