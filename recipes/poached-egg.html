Poached Egg

Description

Poaching eggs is a deceptively complicated task. Thankfully, I have discovered a fool-proof method for making the perfect, teardrop-shaped poached egg every time.

The key to beautiful poached eggs is using enough water, swirling the water before adding the egg, and discarding the outer, less-viscous egg white.

The whole process takes 15 minutes, and yields one poached egg.

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 2 clean ramekins
  • a fine mesh sieve
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 cup of cold water

Steps

  1. Add water and vinegar to a 1-quart saucepan and bring to a boil.
  2. Crack the egg into the fine mesh sieve over the ramekin, allowing the outer egg white to drip through the sieve.
  3. Transfer the egg from the sieve into the other clean ramekin.
  5. Store the excess egg white in the fridge for use in other recipes.
  6. Using a slotted spoon, vigorously swirl the boiling water in the saucepan until it creates a vortex in the middle. (Water depth and velocity of the swirl is key to achieving the teardrop shape)
  7. While the water is still swirling, gently tip the egg out of the ramekin into the water.
  8. Allow the water to come back up to a light simmer.
  9. For runny yolks, cook for 3 minutes. For jammy yolks, cook for 4 minutes.
  10. Remove the egg with the slotted spoon, and dip in cold water to stop the cooking.
  11. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and enjoy!
